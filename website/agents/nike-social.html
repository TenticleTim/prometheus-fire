Mythology

Nike was the winged goddess of victory — she did not cause victory by fighting, she presided over it as its embodiment. She showed up at every contest, every battle, every race, and awarded the laurel to those who had earned it. Her presence marked every win, from the Olympic games to military campaigns. Victory was not possible without her acknowledgement; she was the condition that transformed effort into recognition.

Thesmos channels Nike as social media and community management. Every platform win — a post that spreads, a community that engages, an audience that shows up consistently — requires presence, repetition, and method. Organic reach is not luck; it is earned through platform-native execution, community investment, and content that the algorithm recognizes as generating genuine engagement signals. Nike shows up on every platform and earns the recognition.

Purpose
What Nike does

Nike builds and manages the organic social media presence that compounds through consistent, platform-native execution: 30-day content calendars anchored to the ICP's concentration, post copy written differently for LinkedIn than for X than for Instagram, community management cadences that respond within the algorithm's reward window, and analytics reports that track engagement rate and save rate rather than vanity follower counts. She distinguishes herself from Pheme (PR and press), Apollo (long-form content), and Nike Lead Generation (outbound sequences) — Nike Social owns the organic platform presence.

Responsibilities
Nike's domain
  • Platform-native content calendars: 30-day schedules with post copy adapted for LinkedIn, X/Twitter, and Instagram — never cross-posted identically across platforms
  • LinkedIn content: thought leadership posts, carousels, document posts, and thread structures optimized for the 90-minute engagement window that drives algorithm distribution
  • X/Twitter content: threads (6+ posts), opinionated takes for technical audiences, and hook writing that stops scrolling without being clickbait
  • Instagram content: Reels concepts and scripts, carousel copy, caption writing with GDPR-compliant tracking guidance for UTM parameters
  • Community management playbooks: response SLAs, DM handling protocols, comment engagement cadence, and escalation path for negative interactions
  • Social analytics reporting: engagement rate, save rate, click-through rate, and follower growth — distinguished from vanity metrics (raw likes, follower count alone)
  • Growth experiments: hypothesis, 2-week execution plan, success metric, and kill decision — structured tests, not random posting
