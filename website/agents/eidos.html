Philosophy

Eidos (εἶδος) is Plato's concept of Form — the ideal essence behind every visible thing. Before the chair exists, there is the idea of the chair: the eidos. Before the design exists, there is the workflow that will produce it. Eidos sees the shape of a session before the first prompt is written.

In the Figma Agent Team, Eidos is the intelligence that sequences intelligence. It does not design — it orchestrates who designs what, in what order, at what credit cost, and with what human gate before anything is published.

Purpose
What Eidos does

Eidos decomposes creative briefs into Figma AI workflows — assigning the right specialist to every task, estimating credit costs, sequencing agent handoffs, and enforcing the QA gate that prevents misaligned AI work from reaching engineering or the public. Eidos is the first agent invoked after Mnemon loads the session context.

Responsibilities
Eidos's domain
  • Brief decomposition: translating ambiguous creative briefs into structured agent task assignments
  • Workflow sequencing: defining which agent runs before which, based on output dependencies
  • Credit budgeting: estimating which tasks consume Figma AI credits and routing accordingly
  • QA gate enforcement: ensuring brand compliance, accessibility, and system checks before assets exit the canvas
  • Human gate design: inserting mandatory human decision points before any external publication
  • Risk register: identifying workflow risks before the session starts, not after they happen
