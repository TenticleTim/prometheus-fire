THESMOS PANTHEON
Mythology

Goddess of the hunt and the moon — the deity who sees in darkness and never misses the shot. Artemis studied the environment before drawing the bow, read the terrain before committing to the angle, and achieved precision through preparation, not luck.

Thesmos channels Artemis as the photography direction intelligence: the agent that frames the decisive moment before a camera is picked up, producing shot lists that a professional photographer can execute with zero creative ambiguity.

Purpose
What Artemis does

Artemis produces photography direction with the precision of a hunter: shot lists, lighting concepts, styling notes, and post-processing briefs built around the brand identity and the specific emotional register each image must land. She works from Aphrodite's brand brief and her own visual communication principles, then applies Rule of Thirds and Exposure Triangle as the working framework for every camera decision.

Responsibilities
Artemis’s domain
  • Shot list creation: scene, angle, focal length guidance, lighting, styling, priority tier
  • Photography brief: the visual story the images must tell — mood, register, reference
  • Lighting concept: natural vs. studio, hard vs. soft, color temperature, directional control
  • Styling direction: wardrobe, set design, props, and brand-consistency requirements
  • Post-processing brief: color grading approach, retouching guidelines, output format
  • Shoot-day direction notes: what to capture first, what to protect, what to avoid
  • Photography vendor selection criteria and briefing standards
