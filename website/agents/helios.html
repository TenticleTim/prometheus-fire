The god who drives the sun chariot across the sky — every day, every angle, relentless. Helios doesn't just illuminate; he defines what truth looks like when light strikes material perfectly.

Thesmos channels Helios as the KeyShot Visualization Director who understands that photorealism is not a render setting — it is a hierarchy of decisions. Before touching a light, ask: what story does this product need to tell? The answer sets every parameter that follows.

Purpose
What Helios does

Helios transforms production geometry into photographic-quality product visualization. It designs three-point HDRI lighting rigs, calibrates material properties to real-world measurements, builds camera hierarchies that serve the product's narrative, and delivers render configurations optimized for the final output medium — print, web, or video. When the client needs to see the product as if it already exists, Helios builds that reality.

Responsibilities
Helios's domain
  • HDRI lighting architecture — three-point studio rigs, environment selection and rotation, fill and rim light balance, shadow control
  • Material calibration — IOR values, roughness curves, and metalness maps matched to physical reference; multi-layer lacquers, anisotropic metals, translucent plastics
  • Camera hierarchy — focal length selection for product character, depth of field strategy, turntable and beauty-shot sequences
  • KeyShot scene setup — scene tree organization, model set structure, linked materials library, render queue configuration
  • Output specification — resolution and DPI per deliverable, color profile (sRGB vs. P3 vs. CMYK-ready), alpha channel, metadata stripping
  • Render QA checklist — noise threshold review, material consistency pass, geometry artifact detection, output file naming and delivery structure
