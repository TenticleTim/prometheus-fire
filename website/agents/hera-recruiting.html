Mythology

Hera presided over marriage and formal commitment — the binding contract between parties who were entering a long-term relationship with mutual obligations. She was the goddess of the formal bond, of the covenant that was expected to endure. Where Aphrodite governed attraction and Eros governed desire, Hera governed the deliberate, structured decision to commit.

Thesmos channels Hera as recruiting and hiring. A hire is the most consequential commitment an organization makes — more expensive to undo than to get right, and more disruptive when wrong than most decisions. Hera designs the structured process that makes the right hire consistently, not the inspired gut call that works once. Every interview is a formal process; every offer is a binding commitment.

Purpose
What Hera does

Hera designs the hiring infrastructure upstream of the offer: job descriptions that select for outcomes rather than credentials, interview kits with behavioral anchors that two interviewers score identically, scorecards that survive calibration without debate, and ATS configurations that track the metrics that actually predict hire quality. She operates the process that makes the right hire repeatable — and distinguishes it from Hera Operations, who owns onboarding and everything that happens after the offer is accepted.

Responsibilities
Hera's domain
  • Job description writing: outcome-based requirements, equitable language audit, compensation transparency, and inclusive candidate experience
  • Interview kit design: structured interview guides with behavioral and competency-based questions, mapped to role requirements
  • Scorecard design with behavioral anchors: observable criteria that multiple interviewers score consistently — eliminates subjective "culture fit" language
  • Compensation benchmarking: market data analysis using Levels.fyi, Glassdoor, and Radford, with band recommendations and offer approval matrix
  • ATS configuration: Greenhouse, Lever, and Ashby setup — pipeline stages, scorecards, automated reminders, and EEOC/GDPR compliance fields
  • Candidate experience audit: time-to-response SLAs, communication templates, rejection reasons, and interview logistics
  • Hiring velocity metrics: time-to-fill, offer acceptance rate, interview-to-offer ratio, and source quality tracking

Example deliverables
Job description with outcome-based requirements
Role summary, outcomes for 30/60/90 days, requirements mapped to specific job functions (not years of experience), compensation range, and equitable language audit — selects for capability, not credential proxies.
Interview kit with behavioral question bank
Stage-by-stage interview guide with behavioral and competency-based questions, follow-up probes, and what a strong vs. weak answer looks like for each question — so any interviewer conducts a consistent interview.
Scorecard with behavioral anchors
Competencies mapped to the role, each with 1–5 behavioral anchors that describe observable behaviors at each score level — eliminates the "they seemed great" vs. "they didn't seem right" calibration debate.
