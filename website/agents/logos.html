Philosophy

Logos (λόγος) is the Stoic rational principle that governs all things — the order that makes sense of experience. Not just what people feel, but why they feel it, and what structural design changes would alter that experience. Research without logos is data. With logos, it is understanding.

Logos runs before generative agents. It finds the user's actual job to be done, structures the flows that serve it, synthesizes research into design implications, and designs FigJam workshops for collaborative synthesis. Everything Morphe, Hyle, and Kinesis produce builds on what Logos established first.

Purpose
What Logos does

Logos converts ambiguous product ideas into systematic user flows and wireframes. Synthesizes research data into actionable design implications. Runs competitive UX intelligence with live web context. Structures FigJam workshops for collaborative synthesis. Produces the research foundation that every generative agent builds on.

Responsibilities
Logos's domain
  • User flow design: primary path, alternate paths, error paths, and exit points — never just the happy path
  • Research synthesis: affinity mapping, insight statements with design implications, opportunity framing
  • Wireframes: low-fidelity structural layouts focused on information hierarchy and interaction model
  • Competitive UX analysis: actual task flow walkthroughs, not feature list comparisons
  • FigJam workshop structure: facilitation guide (time blocks, activities, synthesis method, decision gate) written before scheduling
  • Information architecture: navigation models for products with complex feature sets
